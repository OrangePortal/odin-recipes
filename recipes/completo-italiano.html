Completo Italiano

Description

Hot dogs are... not really a thing in Chile. If you gave any Chilean a bun with a sausage, a sauce or two and nothing else, they'd be confused, and a little bit dissapointed.

Not to throw any hate towards regular hot dogs, they taste great, but we Chileans are deeply in love with our Italianos.

Completo means complete or full. Italiano means Italian. You probably get the 'full' part, but, why Italian? Isn't this a Chilean recipe?

Well, let's take a look at the ingredients. First we have the usual bread and sausage, and then we have tomatoes, avocado and mayonnaise. Red, green, and white, the colors of the Italian flag!

So, let's see how we make them.

Ingredients

  • 4 hot dog buns
  • 4 wieners
  • 2 tomatoes
    • strongstrong
  • 2 ripe avocados
  • Mayonnaise
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Optional:
    • Ketchup
    • Mustard

Steps

  1. Bring the sausages to a boil and cook them at medium heat for 5 minutes. Drain and set them apart.
  2. Wash, peel and dice the tomatoes into small pieces. Season them with salt and olive oil.
  3. Cut the avocados in half, unpit them and scoop out the flesh out with a spoon. Mush them and season with salt.
  4. Open the hot dog buns and toast them in a preheated oven at 100° Celsius (212° Fahrenheit) for 2 minutes.
  5. Inside each bun add a sausage, cover a layer of tomato, spread lots of avocado and top with mayonnaise.
  6. Serve the Completo Italiano, add ketchup and mustard if you'd like. Enjoy!
